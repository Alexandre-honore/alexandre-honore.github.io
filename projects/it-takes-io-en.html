It Takes IO

It Takes IO is a local co-op puzzle game built in C++ with SFML. Two players on the same computer embody opposite electrical signals: 0 and 1.

The objective is simple: route a type-1 signal to the level exit gate. To succeed, players must read the circuit, synchronize actions, and manipulate the environment under pressure.

Pitch

Two players, two signals, one circuit to solve. By activating buttons and redirecting flows through logic gates, each room becomes a co-op puzzle where communication matters as much as logic.

Concept

The core gameplay is based on understanding logic gates and real-time cooperation. By standing on buttons, players inject their signal into the level, trigger circuits, and alter connected logic states.

Each level requires finding the right signal path, coordinating timing, and anticipating system reactions.

Main mechanics

  • Two players on the same computer, each as a 0 or 1 signal.
  • Signal routing through cables and logic gates.
  • Movable objects, such as a NOT gate, to solve puzzles.
  • Electrical traps that demand precision and communication.
  • Level objective focused on sending a 1 signal to the exit.

Technical sheet

  • Type: local co-op puzzle game
  • Language: C++
  • Library: SFML
  • Platform: PC, single computer
  • Design focus: electronic logic, cooperation, and circuit readability

What this project highlights

  • A playful translation of electronic logic into interactive level design.
  • Real local co-op gameplay based on timing and communication.
  • Readable but demanding puzzles reinforced by hazards and movable tools.
  • A progression loop centered on circuit understanding.
