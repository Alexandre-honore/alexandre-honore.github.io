Le Nectar des Etoiles

Le Nectar des Etoiles is a short narrative game developed in 48 hours with Unreal Engine during WonderJam Winter 2026 at UQAC. The player takes on the role of a young worker in a space station producing tropical Oasis, during a day that quickly turns unusual.

The project won 1st place at the jam. It focuses on a tense atmosphere, space-station driven art direction, and choice-based narrative designed to make a strong impact quickly.

Pitch

Live an unusual day as a worker in a space station producing tropical Oasis. Between routine, pressure, and decisive choices, each decision brings you closer to a different outcome.

Development context

  • Event: WonderJam Winter 2026 - AEMI / UQAC
  • Duration: 48 hours
  • Engine: Unreal Engine
  • Result: 1st place

Jam themes

The game's concept emerged from combining four imposed themes:

  • Tropical, the jam's main theme
  • Among the stars, a universe set in space
  • The embarrassment of choice, with multiple endings
  • My... Friend? where your ally may also be your enemy

Role in the team

I worked on implementing several game features, but my main role was team coordination and task distribution. I helped keep a clear direction during the jam, organized production, and ensured everyone knew what to deliver and in what order.

This structure was essential to sustain the 48-hour pace and keep gameplay, narrative, and technical production coherent.

Game experience

The core of the project is a short but tense narrative where player choices shape the day and its ending. The space station setting supports a story driven by pressure, trust, and doubt.

The goal was to deliver a memorable experience despite the short production window, relying on a strong core idea, readable staging, and multiple endings to encourage replayability.
